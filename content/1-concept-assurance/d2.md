# Deliverable D2  
## MoSCoW Structure

Deliverable D2 is produced in Step 1 and provides a structured way of scoping the system in terms of its intended capabilities and priorities. It frames the system as a set of desires, organised according to what is essential, desirable, and aspirational within the constraints of technology, time, and budget.

The MoSCoW structure consists of four categories:

- **Must** — What the system is required to achieve at baseline. These are non-negotiable capabilities that define whether the system is viable.

- **Should** — What the system is expected to achieve given realistic constraints such as available technology and budget. These represent strong objectives, but not strictly mandatory for initial operation.

- **Could** — What the system could achieve if additional resources, funding, or capability were available. These are enhancements rather than requirements.

- **Would (or Won’t for now)** — What the system could achieve if given significantly more time or future development, but is explicitly out of scope for the current concept.

D2 is important because it forces an explicit distinction between what is *required for operation* and what is *desirable for performance or future development*. This prevents over-scoping early in the process and provides a clear baseline against which later deliverables — particularly requirements, metrics, and assurance arguments — can be grounded.

---

## Example

For an autonomous mainline railway system, a simplified MoSCoW structure might look like:

- **Must**
  - Safely obey signalling system  
  - Maintain safe braking distances under all conditions  
  - Stop accurately at stations  

- **Should**
  - Optimise journey time within safety constraints  
  - Adapt to minor timetable variations  
  - Provide smooth passenger ride quality  

- **Could**
  - Energy optimisation through driving strategy  
  - Integration with traffic management systems for dynamic routing  

- **Would (Future)**
  - Fully autonomous recovery from all degraded states  
  - Network-wide optimisation across multiple trains
