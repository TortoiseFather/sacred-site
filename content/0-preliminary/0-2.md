
### Purpose

Identify the key actors involved in the system and define how they interact.

This establishes who is responsible for decisions, how information flows, and where authority sits before any technical design begins.

---

### What is captured

At this stage, SACRED focuses on the **core operational relationships**, for example:

- Train ↔ Signalling (movement authority, constraints)  
- Train ↔ Control/Dispatch (service coordination, disruption handling)  
- Train ↔ Onboard Staff (supervision, intervention)  

These are not fully detailed yet — they are recorded at a high level to ensure the system is grounded in the real railway ecosystem.

---

### Why it matters

Stakeholder relationships define:

- who makes decisions  
- who provides information  
- who is responsible when things go wrong  

Without this, later steps risk making incorrect assumptions about autonomy, responsibility, and system behaviour.

---

### Output

A minimal set of **explicit stakeholder relationships** that:

- anchor the system within the railway ecosystem  
- make authority and communication pathways visible  
- provide a foundation for hazard analysis and responsibility allocation in later steps
