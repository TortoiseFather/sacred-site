
Step 6.2 defines what technologies can and cannot do.  
Step 6.3 defines when those technologies can be relied upon.

We understand hazards to be state-dependent, a capability that is sufficient in one state may be ineffective in another.

Operational State Validation ensures that each technology is only used in operational contexts where its assumptions remain valid.

---

### Purpose

Link technologies to the operational states of the ODM, ensuring that:

- capabilities are only used where they remain valid,
- hazards are mitigated by appropriate mechanisms,
- and responsibility is correctly distributed between the ego system and the wider ecosystem.

---

### Activities

For each operational state:

1. Identify the hazards that remain relevant in that state.
2. Determine which technologies are responsible for detection or mitigation.
3. Check that the assumptions from SotA TRAP still hold in that context.
4. Identify where technologies become unreliable or unavailable.
5. Assign alternative mitigation (e.g. degraded behaviour or ecosystem control).

---

### Example: Adhesion Across States

Adhesion affects braking performance, but its impact depends on the operational state.

- **Cruise**  
  Adhesion uncertainty has limited immediate impact.  
  The system can monitor conditions and prepare for changes.

- **Signal Approach**  
  Adhesion becomes safety-critical.  
  The ability to stop within the Signal Visibility Budget depends directly on braking performance.

- **Station Approach**  
  Similar constraints apply, but with tighter stopping requirements and lower speeds.

From Step 6.2, adhesion monitoring depends on maintaining a reliable estimate of braking capability.  
Here, we validate where that assumption holds:

- If adhesion can be estimated reliably → normal operation continues  
- If adhesion is uncertain → transition to conservative braking  
- If adhesion cannot be bounded → rely on degraded modes or ecosystem constraints (e.g. speed restrictions)

---

### Role in the Methodology

The output of Step 6.3 is a mapping between:

- **Operational States (ODM)**  
- **Relevant Hazards**  
- **Supporting Technologies**

This mapping defines:

- where autonomy is technically supported,  
- where it requires degraded behaviour,  
- and where responsibility shifts to the railway ecosystem.

These constraints are carried forward into [**Step 6.5 (Boundary Definition)**](/#/steps/6-design-assurance/examples/6-3) and ultimately define the conditions of the [Safe Operating Concept](/#/steps/7-soc).
