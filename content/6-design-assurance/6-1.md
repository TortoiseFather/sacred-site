## 6.1 Technology Scoping

### Purpose

Identify whether the capabilities required by the SACRED safety artefacts can be supported by existing or emerging technologies.

This step connects the requirements, metrics and failure responses to real systems, ensuring the Safe Operating Concept is grounded in what can be practically delivered.

### Activities

1. Extract required capabilities from the Assurance Traceability Matrix and metrics.
2. Group capabilities into functional areas (e.g. perception, localisation, communication, control).
3. Review railway and adjacent technologies that demonstrate these capabilities.
4. Compare technology performance against required safety bounds.
5. Classify capabilities as supported, partially supported, or unsupported.
6. Record representative systems to support later assessment.
7. Feed constraints back into the Safe Operating Concept.

### Justification

SACRED defines safety in terms of measurable bounds and predefined responses. These depend on specific technical capabilities.

Technology Scoping ensures those capabilities are realistic by linking requirements to demonstrated systems. It also exposes where assumptions depend on immature or incomplete technology, allowing operational boundaries to be set accordingly.

### Example: Adhesion and Stopping Capability

One of the core constraints identified in earlier steps is braking performance under low adhesion.

Adhesion directly affects stopping distance. As adhesion decreases, the train requires more distance to stop, increasing the minimum signal sighting requirement. This relationship is captured in the Signal Visibility Budget (SVB), where lower adhesion increases the required reaction distance.

In the Swalwell case study, the required sighting distance ranges from 80–160 m depending on adhesion conditions, while the actual signal becomes visible at approximately 200 m. Under nominal conditions, this is sufficient.

The question in Step 6 is whether available technology can support this assumption.

This introduces three capability checks:

- **Detection**: Can the system reliably identify the signal at ≥160 m under real conditions (rain, glare, occlusion)?
- **Braking awareness**: Can the system detect degraded adhesion (e.g. slip/slide, braking mismatch) in real time?
- **Control response**: Can the system adapt braking behaviour conservatively when adhesion is uncertain?
