## 7.3 Safe Operating Concept

Step 7.3 presents the final output of SACRED:

> **The Safe Operating Concept (SOC)**

This is a compact set of conditions that define when autonomous operation is valid.

- If all conditions hold → autonomy is permitted  
- If any condition fails → autonomy is withdrawn and fallback behaviour applies  

---

## D19: Safe Operating Concept — Newcastle Corridor

Autonomous operation on the Swalwell Junction–Newcastle corridor is permitted only if all of the following conditions are satisfied:

---

### Domain and Authority

1. The system operates within the defined corridor and direction of travel.  
2. Signalling infrastructure remains authoritative for movement permission.  

---

### Speed and Control

3. Train speed remains within the defined infrastructure speed profile.  

---

### Signal Approach (SVB)

4. During signal approach, available sighting distance satisfies the Signal Visibility Budget.  
5. Reaction time assumptions used in calibration remain valid.  

---

### Braking and Adhesion

6. Braking capability is defined using a conservative lower bound on adhesion.  
7. Adhesion integrity is maintained, or speed is reduced to preserve braking safety.  

---

### Authority Resolution

8. Movement authority is resolved within the required decision window.  

---

### Hazard Detection

9. Hazard detection capability is consistent with stopping requirements,  
   or equivalent mitigation is provided through reduced speed or ecosystem support.  

---

### System Integrity

10. No critical event is active that invalidates the operating envelope.  
11. The system maintains an auditable record of decisions and system state.  
12. Perception systems are used only within validated environmental and operational limits.  

---

## Enforcement

If any condition is not satisfied:

> The system shall withdraw the autonomy claim and transition to the defined degraded or minimum-risk behaviour.
