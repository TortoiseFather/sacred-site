# Deliverable D19  
## Safe Operating Concept (SOC)

Deliverable D19 represents the final output of the SACRED methodology: the **Safe Operating Concept (SOC)**. It consolidates the results of all previous steps into a clear, formal statement of **when autonomous operation may be safely claimed**.

D19 defines the **operational boundary of autonomy**, specifying the exact conditions under which the system is considered safe to operate, and by implication, when it is not.

---

## Purpose

D19 answers the overarching question:

> **Under what conditions is autonomous operation safe and justifiable?**

It does this by bringing together:
- Scenario definition and operational structure (D1, D7)  
- Validated hazards and requirements (D8–D9)  
- Quantified safety constraints (D11–D14)  
- Critical event handling (D15, D17)  
- Feasibility assessment (D18)  

---

## Key Characteristics

- **Explicit and bounded**  
  Clearly defines the limits within which autonomy is valid  

- **Condition-based**  
  Expressed as a set of enforceable operational conditions  

- **Traceable**  
  Every condition can be linked back to hazards, requirements, and metrics  

- **Actionable**  
  Directly informs system behaviour, operational procedures, and assurance arguments  

---

## Structure

The Safe Operating Concept typically includes:

- **Preconditions for autonomous operation**  
  Derived from D16 (Scoped SOC)  

- **Admissibility constraints**  
  From D14 (Unified Metrics)  

- **Failure handling and degraded states**  
  From D15 (Criticality) and D17 (TRAP Table)  

- **Feasibility considerations**  
  From D18 (State-of-the-Art Assessment)  

Together, these define a **complete operational contract** for autonomy.

---

## Example

A simplified SOC might state:

- Autonomous operation is permitted only if:
  - Signal visibility and braking constraints are satisfied  
  - Environmental conditions remain within defined bounds  
  - No critical event class is active  
  - System integrity assumptions hold  

- If any condition is violated:
  - The system must transition to a predefined degraded state  
  - Or withdraw autonomy entirely
