# Deliverable D6  
## State Map (State-Conditioned Hazard Pathways)

Deliverable D6 is produced at the end of Step 2 and captures **representative hazard pathways in a state-conditioned form**. It builds directly on the fault trees (D4) and Eco–Ego assignments (D5), providing a more detailed and operationally grounded view of how hazards unfold over time.

Where D4 identifies causal structure at a high level, D6 introduces **state and progression**, showing how hazards emerge through sequences of conditions and transitions within the system.

---

## Purpose

D6 makes explicit:

- Which **assumptions are made about the ecosystem**  
- Which **failure avenues are realised at the ego-vehicle**  
- Where **additional contextual information must be represented in the ODM**  

This provides the necessary grounding for:
- Requirement derivation in Step 3  
- Failure management and procedural mitigation in Step 5  

In effect, D6 bridges the gap between *causal explanation* (D4) and *operational handling* (later steps).

---

## Key Characteristics

- Represents hazards as **state-conditioned pathways**, not isolated events  
- Builds on fully identified and classified components from D4 and D5  
- Captures **how conditions evolve over time**, rather than just why they occur  
- Highlights where the ODM must encode additional **context or variables**  
- Focuses on **representative pathways**, not exhaustive enumeration  

D6 can be thought of as a **higher-resolution version of D4**, where causal relationships are embedded within operational states and transitions.

---

## Structure

A typical D6 pathway includes:

- **Initial operational state**  
- **Ecosystem conditions and assumptions**  
- **Ego-vehicle state and capabilities**  
- **Sequence of transitions or degradations**  
- **Hazard realisation point**  

This allows the analyst to trace how a system moves from nominal operation into a hazardous condition under specific contextual assumptions.

---

## Example

For a hazard such as **H-01: SPAD**, a simplified state-conditioned pathway might look like:

1. **Normal running state**  
   - Train operating within speed limits  
   - Signal ahead is red  

2. **Ecosystem condition**  
   - Reduced adhesion due to environmental factors  
   - Signal visibility partially degraded  

3. **Ego state transition**  
   - Signal detected late  
   - Braking initiated later than nominal  

4. **Degraded braking state**  
   - Available braking performance reduced  
   - Stopping distance exceeds available sighting distance  

5. **Hazard realisation**  
   - Train passes signal at danger  

This pathway shows not just the causes of the hazard, but **how those causes interact over time within the operational context**.

---

D6 therefore transforms hazard analysis from a static structure into a **dynamic, state-aware representation**, enabling more precise requirements and more realistic mitigation strategies in subsequent steps.
