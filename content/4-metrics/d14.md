# Deliverable D14  
## Unified Metrics (Admissibility Conditions)

Deliverable D14 is produced in Step 4 and represents the **composition of all prior metric work** into a unified, operational form.

It combines:
- **D11:** Requirement-based metric forms  
- **D12:** Ego-vehicle capability bounds  
- **D13:** Route-specific environmental constraints  

to produce **state-conditioned admissibility predicates** — the conditions under which the system is allowed to operate safely.

---

## Purpose

D14 transforms earlier qualitative and semi-quantitative analysis into a **concrete, checkable set of conditions** that define safe operation in practice.

For each operational state defined in the ODM, D14:
- Instantiates symbolic metric expressions  
- Calibrates them with real values  
- Produces inequalities or logical conditions that must hold  

These conditions define the **admissible operating envelope** of the system.

---

## Key Characteristics

- **State-conditioned**  
  Metrics are tied to specific operational states (e.g. signal approach, braking, normal running)

- **Compositional**  
  Integrates system capability (D12) with environmental demand (D13)

- **Quantitative and checkable**  
  Expressed as inequalities or logical conditions that can be evaluated at runtime

- **Operationally meaningful**  
  Directly determines whether the system can continue nominal operation or must transition state

---

## Role in the Methodology

D14 is the point at which SACRED produces **runtime-relevant safety constraints**.

It directly informs:
- **Safe Operating Concept (D16)**  
- **Failure management and state transitions (Step 5)**  
- **State-of-the-art capability evaluation (Step 6)**  

D14 provides the **quantitative substrate** upon which these later steps depend.

---

## Example

For a signal approach state, a unified metric might take the form:

- The available sighting distance must be greater than or equal to:
  - Reaction distance (based on perception and latency)  
  - Plus stopping distance (based on braking capability)  
  - Plus a safety margin  

This results in a condition such as:

- *Safe operation is admissible only if the train can stop within the visible distance ahead*

If this condition is violated, the system must:
- Transition to a degraded state, or  
- Trigger a predefined safety response
