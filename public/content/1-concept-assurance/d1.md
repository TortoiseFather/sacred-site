# Deliverable D1  
## Initial Scenario Definition

Deliverable D1 is produced in Step 1 and acts as the initial scenario definition for the proposed system. It takes the abstract concept introduced in D0 and places it into the operating domain described by the [ODM](ref:(#/steps/1-concept-assurance/examples/1-e)). Whereas the ODM records what the environment exposes — its bounds, interfaces, and variables — D1 specifies how the system is expected to progress through that environment during routine service.

In practical terms, D1 defines:
- The assumed service pattern  
- The nominal sequence of operational modes  
- The milestones that trigger transitions between modes  

These milestones might include events such as leaving a station, approaching a signal, entering a junction, reaching a braking point, or beginning a platform approach. D1 therefore describes what *normal progression* looks like, before hazards, degraded behaviour, and fallback responses are introduced in later steps.

D1 is important because the railway is not a static environment; it structures behaviour over space and time. The same route exposes different constraints at different points, D1 provides the first operational description of how the autonomous system is expected to respond to them in sequence.

---

## Example

For a section such as **Swalwell Junction → MetroCentre**, D1 might describe a nominal progression such as:

1. Initial state (system checks / readiness)  
2. Departure from station  
3. Acceleration into normal running  
4. Speed stabilisation under route limits  
5. Approach phase triggered by upcoming station or signal  
6. Braking phase  
7. Platform approach and stopping
