
Deliverable D11 is produced in Step 4 and translates the qualitative safety requirements derived in Step 3 into **measurable, state-conditioned metric forms**.

Where earlier deliverables define *what the system must achieve*, D11 defines **how this can be measured and evaluated in practice**.

---

## Purpose

D11 bridges the gap between:
- **Qualitative requirements** (e.g. “the system shall stop before a red signal”)  
and  
- **Quantitative evaluation** (e.g. measurable conditions on speed, distance, and braking performance)

It ensures that every requirement can be:
- Observed  
- Measured  
- Verified  

within the operational context defined by the ODM.

---

## Process

For each requirement:

1. **Identify the relevant operational state**  
   (e.g. signal approach, normal running, braking phase)

2. **Extract observable variables**  
   These may include:
   - Speed  
   - Distance to target (e.g. signal or station)  
   - Braking capability  
   - Environmental conditions  

3. **Define measurable conditions ("ascertains")**  
   Each requirement is expressed as one or more **ascertains** — explicit, testable conditions that must hold true during operation.

---

## Key Characteristics

- Metrics are **state-conditioned**, not global  
- Derived directly from validated hazards and requirements (D8–D9)  
- Expressed in terms of **observable variables**  
- Designed to support **real-time evaluation and assurance**  

D11 therefore transforms requirements from abstract statements into **operationally testable constraints**.

---

## Example

For a requirement such as:

> The system shall stop before a red signal

D11 might define metrics such as:

- **State:** Signal approach  
- **Variables:**  
  - Train speed (v)  
  - Distance to signal (d)  
  - Available braking deceleration (a)  

- **Ascertain:**  
  - The available stopping distance must be less than or equal to the distance to the signal  

This can be interpreted as ensuring that, at any point during the approach, the system remains within a **safe braking envelope**.

---

D11 therefore provides the foundation for all subsequent analysis by ensuring that safety requirements are expressed in a form that is **measurable, testable, and grounded in the operational environment**.
