# Deliverable D16  
## Scoped Safe Operating Concept (SOC)

Deliverable D16 is produced at the end of Step 4 and defines the **Scoped Safe Operating Concept (SOC)** — a compact, operational statement of when autonomy may be safely claimed.

It consolidates the calibrated admissibility conditions from D14 into a **clear set of preconditions** that must hold for autonomous operation to be permitted.

---

## Purpose

D16 answers a simple but critical question:

> **Under what conditions is autonomous operation allowed?**

It does this by expressing safety constraints as a **checklist of conditions**, each derived from:
- Requirement-based metrics (D11)  
- Ego capability bounds (D12)  
- Route-specific constraints (D13)  
- Unified admissibility predicates (D14)  

---

## Key Characteristics

- **Compact and operational**  
  Presented as a concise checklist (typically ~10 conditions)

- **State-aware**  
  Conditions are tied to the operational states defined in the ODM

- **Route-specific**  
  Grounded in the actual constraints of the defined area of operation

- **Binary in interpretation**  
  Each condition either holds or does not  

---

## Gating Function

D16 acts as a **gate for autonomy**:

- If **all conditions hold** → autonomous operation is permitted  
- If **any condition fails** → the autonomy claim is withdrawn  

This makes the SOC a **clear operational contract**, defining the boundary between:
- Nominal autonomous operation  
- Degraded operation or intervention  

---

## Role in the Methodology

D16 is the first point where SACRED produces a **usable operational artefact**.

It serves as:
- The basis for **runtime decision-making**  
- An input to **failure management (Step 5)**  
- A foundation for **state-of-the-art evaluation (Step 6)**  

Later steps refine and enforce this concept, but D16 provides the initial, consolidated statement.

---

## Example

A simplified SOC checklist might include conditions such as:

- Signal visibility is within required sighting distance  
- Braking capability is sufficient for current speed and conditions  
- No critical event class (D15) is active  
- Movement authority is clearly defined and consistent  
- Environmental conditions remain within defined bounds  

If any of these conditions are violated, the system must:
- Exit autonomous mode, or  
- Transition to a predefined degraded state
