
Deliverable D13 is produced in Step 4 and captures the **environmental quantities** that constrain safe operation, independent of the ego-vehicle’s design.

Where D12 defines what the system *can do*, D13 defines what the **route demands**.

---

## Purpose

D13 records measurable properties of the operational corridor that directly affect safety, derived from:

- Physical inspection  
- Route mapping  
- Infrastructure and signalling documentation  

These quantities represent constraints that the system must operate within, regardless of its internal capabilities.

---

## Key Characteristics

D13 captures **fixed and contextual environmental features**, including:

- **Geometric distances**  
  - Signal spacing  
  - Sighting distances  
  - Distances to stations, junctions, and key infrastructure  

- **Infrastructure constraints**  
  - Speed limits  
  - Signalling layouts  
  - Track geometry and gradients  

- **Structural and environmental features**  
  - Overpasses and bridges causing partial occlusion  
  - Areas with known visibility constraints  
  - Sections prone to environmental effects (e.g. low adhesion zones)  

These values are **independent of the ego-vehicle**, and represent the external conditions under which the system must function.

---

## Role in the Methodology

D13 forms the environmental counterpart to D12:

- **D12 (Ego):** System capability bounds  
- **D13 (Environment):** Route-imposed constraints  

These are later combined in:
- **D14 (Unified Metrics)** → to determine whether the system can safely operate under given conditions  

If D12 defines *what is possible*, D13 defines *what is required*.

---

## Example

For a signal approach scenario, D13 might include:

- Distance from signal to preceding point on track  
- Minimum sighting distance due to track curvature or obstructions  
- Speed limit for the section  
- Presence of an overpass reducing visibility  

These values define the **external constraints** within which braking, perception, and decision-making must occur.
