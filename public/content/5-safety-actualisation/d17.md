
Deliverable D17 is produced in Step 5 and defines the **TRAP Table**, which operationalises failure management within the SACRED methodology.

It specifies, for each identified failure mode and critical event class, the **predefined degraded behaviour** required to transition the system from a state where safe operation can no longer be assured to a **Minimum Risk State (MRS)**.

---

## Purpose

D17 answers the question:

> **What does the system do when safe operation is no longer guaranteed?**

It ensures that:
- Failures are handled **deterministically**, not reactively  
- Transitions to safety are **predefined and auditable**  
- The system always moves toward a **known, acceptable state**  

---

## TRAP Structure

Each entry in the TRAP Table is defined by four components:

- **Trigger**  
  A checkable condition indicating that safe operation is no longer admissible  
  (e.g. violation of a D14 metric or loss of an integrity assumption)

- **Response**  
  The immediate action taken to mitigate the hazard  
  (e.g. initiate braking, restrict speed, halt movement)

- **Assurance**  
  The target **Minimum Risk State (MRS)** the system must achieve  
  (e.g. safe stop before hazard, controlled halt at a station)

- **Process**  
  The procedural steps required to:
  - Ensure auditability  
  - Coordinate with the wider ecosystem (e.g. signalling, operators)  
  - Maintain system traceability  

---

## Key Characteristics

- Derived from:
  - Critical event classes (D15)  
  - Admissibility conditions (D14)  

- Defines **pre-planned responses**, not real-time improvisation  

- Ensures **traceable and repeatable behaviour** under failure  

- Bridges the gap between:
  - Metric violation  
  - Safe system state  

---

## Role in the Methodology

D17 is the core output of **Step 5 (Failure Management)**.

It connects:
- **Detection of unsafe conditions** → via D14  
- **Classification of critical events** → via D15  
- **Execution of safe responses** → via predefined TRAP entries  



---

## Example

A simplified TRAP entry might look like:

- **Trigger:**  
  Braking distance exceeds available sighting distance (D14 violation)

- **Response:**  
  Initiate maximum service or emergency braking  

- **Assurance:**  
  Train comes to a complete stop before entering unsafe zone  

- **Process:**  
  Log event, notify signalling system, maintain stopped state until conditions are reassessed
