# Deliverable D8  
## Validated Hazard Register

Deliverable D8 is produced in Step 3 and represents the **validated Hazard Register**. It is an updated version of D3, where previously identified hazards are reviewed and confirmed against the operational context, available evidence, and system assumptions.

Where D3 captures *what could go wrong*, D8 captures **what remains credibly hazardous after validation**.

Each hazard in D8 forms a traceable link between:
- The scenario definition (D1)  
- The Operational Design Model (D7)  
- The causal structures identified in Step 2 (D4–D6)  
- Future requirements and assurance evidence  

---

## Purpose

D8 is important because it:
- Filters out speculative or non-relevant hazards  
- Anchors hazards to **realistic operational conditions**  
- Introduces **traceability** into the assurance process  
- Provides the foundation for requirement derivation in Step 3  

Unlike the exploratory nature of fault trees (D4), D8 represents a **committed set of hazards** that the system must address.

---

## Structure

The Validated Hazard Register is typically structured as a database or table, with each entry containing:

- **Hazard ID**  
- **Concept / Name**  
- **Description**  
- **Eco/Ego Classification**  
- **Detection Method (how the hazard is identified in operation)**  
- **Supporting Source or Evidence**  

This ensures that each hazard is not only defined, but also **observable and justifiable**.

---

## Example

A simplified representation of the validated hazard register might include:

- **H-01: SPAR (Signal Passed At Red)**  
  - A train passes a red signal without authorisation, risking collision or route conflict.  
  - Causes may include visual occlusion, signal misinterpretation, or insufficient braking.  
  - **Eco/Ego:** Both  
  - **Detection:** To be defined  
  - **Source:** Network Rail Sighting Handbook  

- **H-02: SPAD (Signal Passed At Danger)**  
  - A SPAR formally classified following an incident or rule-based determination.  
  - **Eco/Ego:** Ecosystem  
  - **Detection:** Determined by operational and signalling context  
  - **Source:** Network Rail Signalling Handbook  

- **H-03: Low Adhesion**  
  - Reduction in wheel–rail adhesion due to environmental factors such as leaf buildup, moisture, or ice, increasing stopping distance.  
  - **Eco/Ego:** Both (primarily ecosystem-driven, ego-managed)  
  - **Detection:** Onboard wheel-slip sensors and traction control feedback  
  - **Source:** Network Rail guidance, RAIB reports, stakeholder input  

---

D8 transforms the hazard register from an exploratory list into a **validated, traceable foundation** for the remainder of the SACRED methodology.
