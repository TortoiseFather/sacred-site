# Deliverable D10  
## Limitation Report

Deliverable D10 is produced at the end of Step 3 and records the outcome of Steps 1–3 in a structured, narrative form. It documents how **assurance boundaries emerge in practice** for the defined scenario, and explains why certain hazards impose fundamental limits on what the autonomous system can safely claim to achieve.

Rather than introducing new artefacts, D10 consolidates the preceding work — scenario definition, hazard analysis, requirement derivation, and traceability — into a clear statement of **where and why autonomy is bounded**.

---

## Purpose

D10 serves two primary roles:

### 1. Defining Assurance Boundaries

D10 explicitly identifies the conditions under which:
- Nominal requirements are **no longer valid**  
- Safe operation can **no longer be guaranteed**  
- The system must transition to **degraded operation** or **withdraw autonomy**  

These boundaries are not arbitrary — they arise from:
- Physical constraints (e.g. braking limits, visibility)  
- Organisational constraints (e.g. signalling practices, authority structures)  
- Informational constraints (e.g. uncertainty, incomplete perception)  

---

### 2. Informing Metric Definition (Step 4)

D10 provides a direct input to Step 4 by highlighting where:
- Performance cannot be defined purely in nominal terms  
- Metrics must instead capture **uncertainty, integrity, and bounded confidence**  

This ensures that later quantitative definitions reflect **real operational limits**, not idealised behaviour.

---

## Key Characteristics

- Written in **narrative form**, rather than as a table or model  
- Grounded in the outputs of D1–D9  
- Explicitly identifies **limiting factors and assumptions**  
- Links hazards to **practical constraints on autonomy**  
- Establishes the conditions for **state transitions and fallback behaviour**  

D10 therefore acts as the point where the methodology shifts from *what is required* to *what is realistically supportable*.
