# Deliverable D9  
## Assurance Traceability Matrix (ATM)

Deliverable D9 is produced in Step 3 and defines the **Assurance Traceability Matrix (ATM)**. It provides a structured mapping between validated hazards, the requirements introduced to mitigate them, and the artefacts or domain features through which those requirements are satisfied.

In essence, the ATM captures a **three-way relationship** between:
- **Hazards** (from D8)  
- **Obligations / Requirements** (derived in Step 3)  
- **Supporting elements** (from the ODM or external artefacts)  

---

## Purpose

The ATM is central to assurance because it ensures that:

- Every validated hazard is **explicitly addressed**  
- Each requirement has a **clear justification**  
- All requirements are grounded in **real system elements or evidence**  

It prevents gaps where hazards are identified but not mitigated, or where requirements exist without a clear safety purpose.

---

## Structure

At its simplest, each entry in the ATM links:

- **Hazard ID**  
- **Requirement(s)** addressing that hazard  
- **Supporting artefact or ODM feature** that enables or enforces the requirement  

This can be extended to include:
- Evidence sources  
- Verification methods  
- Responsibility (ego / ecosystem)  

The result is a **traceable chain** from hazard → requirement → implementation context.

---

## Example

A simplified ATM might include entries such as:

- **H-01: SPAD**  
  - **Requirement:** The system shall detect and respond to a red signal within the available sighting distance  
  - **Supported by:** Signal sighting distance (ODM), onboard perception system  

- **H-03: Low Adhesion**  
  - **Requirement:** The system shall maintain braking performance within defined limits under reduced adhesion conditions  
  - **Supported by:** Adhesion estimates (ODM), braking control system  

- **H-11: Authority Ambiguity**  
  - **Requirement:** The system shall maintain a consistent and verified interpretation of movement authority  
  - **Supported by:** Signalling interface (ODM), communication protocols  

---

The ATM therefore acts as the **backbone of assurance traceability**, linking hazards to the concrete mechanisms through which they are mitigated.
