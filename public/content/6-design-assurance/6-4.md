
Step 6.3 defines where technologies are valid.  
Step 6.4 checks whether those assumptions hold when applied in practice.

This step introduces simple simulation and experimentation to observe how technologies behave under representative conditions.

---

### Purpose

Test whether the assumptions identified in earlier steps remain consistent with observed system behaviour.

This focuses on questions such as:

- Do sensing ranges match what is required by the safety metrics?
- Do control responses behave as expected under degraded conditions?
- Do technologies remain reliable within the operational states they are assigned to?

---

### Activities

1. Select critical assumptions from SotA TRAP and Operational State Validation.
2. Construct simplified scenarios based on the ODM.
3. Apply representative technologies (or proxies) within those scenarios.
4. Observe behaviour under nominal and degraded conditions.
5. Compare observed performance against required safety bounds.
6. Record mismatches and feed them into boundary definition.

---

### Example: Perception Distance vs Braking Requirement

From earlier steps:

- The Signal Visibility Budget defines the distance required to safely detect and react to a signal.
- Adhesion determines braking performance and therefore required stopping distance.

A key assumption is that perception systems can detect signals or hazards at sufficient range.

In a simplified experiment:

- Off-the-shelf vision models were tested for distance estimation and object detection.
- Performance varied significantly and degraded under realistic conditions.
- Reliable detection at the distances required by the safety metrics was not consistently observed.

This creates a mismatch:

- **Required**: consistent detection at distances aligned with braking constraints  
- **Observed**: detection performance limited and variable  

### Role in the Methodology

The output of Step 6.4 is evidence:

- confirming where assumptions are valid,
- identifying where they fail,
- and refining the limits of each technology.

These findings feed directly into [**Step 6.5 (Boundary Definition)**](/#/steps/6-design-assurance/examples/6-5), where operational constraints are finalised and the Safe Operating Concept is shaped around observed capability.
