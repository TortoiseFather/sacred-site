
Deliverable D18 is produced in Step 6 and provides a **State-of-the-Art Capability Assessment**. It evaluates whether existing technologies, as demonstrated in research and real-world deployment, can plausibly support the Safe Operating Concept (D16).

D18 acts as a **feasibility audit**, testing whether the assumptions made throughout the SACRED process are grounded in what is currently achievable.

---

## Purpose

D18 answers the question:

> **Can the Safe Operating Concept actually be realised with current technology?**

It does this by assessing each key assumption and requirement against:
- Published research  
- Industrial systems  
- Demonstrated capabilities in practice  

---

## Key Outcomes

For each assumption or requirement underpinning the SOC, D18 classifies it as:

- **Supported**  
  Backed by demonstrated, reliable technology in comparable contexts  

- **Partially Supported / Uncertain**  
  Some evidence exists, but limitations, gaps, or scaling issues remain  

- **Unsupported (Requires Further Development)**  
  No convincing evidence that current technology can meet the requirement  

- **Ecosystem-Dependent**  
  Relies on external systems (e.g. signalling infrastructure, operators) rather than onboard autonomy  

---

## Key Characteristics

- Grounded in **real-world capability**, not theoretical possibility  
- Distinguishes between **ego-vehicle capability** and **ecosystem support**  
- Highlights **technology gaps and risks**  
- Provides evidence for **assurance arguments and limitations**  

---

## Role in the Methodology

D18 forms the bridge between:
- **Conceptual assurance (Steps 1–5)**  
and  
- **Practical feasibility (Step 6)**  

It informs:
- Whether the SOC can be claimed with confidence  
- Where assumptions must be relaxed or constrained  
- What additional development or evidence is required  

---

## Example

For a requirement such as reliable long-range signal detection, D18 might conclude:

- **Partially Supported**  
  - Short-range detection is well demonstrated  
  - Long-range detection under all conditions (e.g. weather, occlusion) remains uncertain  

For adhesion estimation:

- **Supported (with limitations)**  
  - Real-time estimation is feasible in controlled settings  
  - Performance at very low adhesion levels or high speeds may be less certain  

For emergency handling:

- **Ecosystem-Dependent**  
  - Safe resolution may rely on signalling systems or operator intervention
